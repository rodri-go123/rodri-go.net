Experimental Handbook for Internet Autonomy

The Experimental Handbook for Internet Autonomy is a Zine, and the physical counterpart of The Autonomous Web. It takes the experiments and behaviours of the speculative Autonomous Web and turns them into executable ‘human algorithms’, making them tangible tools for collaborative (re)thinking about our relationship to the network.

Cymatics: Light-mapping

Part of a bigger project about Cymatics, Light-mapping uses a laser and sound vibrations to visualise music and propose an alternative to the much overused band tee with a big logo on it. Instead of sporting your H&M Nirvana shirt to your next Foo Fighters show, why not chose to show your fandom in a more intimate way? This brings us to the concept of the light-map as a restricted signifier (in opposition to a universal signifier). The image itself is an accurate data-visualisation of any fragment of music, however only the person wearing it knows the exact meaning.

The examples above feature The Prodigy, as a tribute to Keith Flint, and David Bowie.

See whole project.

2019: A Media Space Odyssey

2019: A Media Space Odyssey is a tripartite anthology of fictional and real world stories where the voice of minorities is brought into question. This hybrid publication manifests itself both in physical and virtual space, and features stories from Hao Jinfang, Hong Kong, Chile and Qatar. Besides serving as a physical platform and archive for voices of real people that don’t get representation in the media, this publication shows the many ways the third world still gets censored by the first world in an age of information overflow.

Read about the web publication.

The Cloud is Clouded

What exactly is The Cloud?
This hybrid visual essay takes shape in print and video format. It’s the conclusion of research into how we perceive the symbology of clouds throughout history and how our relationship with the modern Cloud is evolving.

Koen Tasselaar Meets

Two by one meter poster analysing “How to Maintain a Balanced Lifestyle While Keeping Your Eyes on the Grid” by dutch artist Koen Taselaar, and comparing it to other artists in and outside the design field: Radiohead, Yayoi Kusama, Iris Van Herpen, Metahaven, Rafael Rozendaal, Escher and Gilles de Brock.

Exhibited at the Palace of Typographic Masonry

Made in collaboration with Annabel Kers, Jessy Richter and Reinier de Waal
